# Personal Tone Guidelines

Use this tone when writing as an individual developer sharing something they built or discovered. Not a corporate announcement — a person talking to other developers.

## Voice Characteristics

- **First person singular**: "I built", "I was frustrated by", "I found that"
- **Direct**: Skip preamble. Start with what's interesting.
- **Conversational but precise**: Use plain language. Technical terms are fine when accurate.
- **Genuine enthusiasm**: It's okay to say something is cool. Avoid marketing superlatives.
- **Honest about trade-offs**: Mention limitations when they're relevant.

## What to Do

- Open with the problem or the insight, not the feature name
- Use concrete examples over abstract descriptions
- Share the "why I built this" or "what surprised me" angle when relevant
- Write like you're telling a colleague over coffee, not presenting at a conference

## What to Avoid

- "Excited to announce" — just announce it
- "Game-changing", "revolutionary", "paradigm-shifting" — too much
- Passive voice: "it was decided" → "I decided"
- Corporate "we" when you mean "I"
- Emoji inflation (one or two is fine; five is a newsletter, not a tweet)
- Listing every feature — pick the one thing that matters most

## Example Transformations

**Before (corporate):**
> We are thrilled to announce the release of structured output validation in Mellea v0.9. This powerful new capability enables developers to ensure LLM outputs conform to Pydantic schemas with automatic retry logic.

**After (personal):**
> Built something I've wanted for a while: Mellea now validates LLM outputs against a Pydantic model and retries automatically if the structure is wrong. Took three lines to add to an existing project.

## Platform-Specific Notes

- **Twitter/X**: One strong observation or demo. Don't try to explain everything.
- **LinkedIn**: Can be slightly longer and more reflective, but keep the personal voice.
