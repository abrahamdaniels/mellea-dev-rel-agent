# LinkedIn Conventions

Platform rules and norms for posting on LinkedIn as part of Mellea developer relations.

## Character Limits

- **Post body**: ~3,000 characters (posts longer than this are truncated)
- **Post headline**: ~120 characters (shown in shares)
- **"See more" fold**: Approximately the first 2-3 lines (around 210 characters) are visible before the fold

## The Critical First 2-3 Lines

LinkedIn hides everything after the fold until the reader clicks "see more". Your opening lines must work on their own and create enough curiosity to earn the click.

- Lead with the insight, problem, or claim — not the background
- Avoid "I'm excited to share..." as the opening — it conveys nothing and wastes the fold
- Good opening: the most interesting sentence in your post

## Post Structure

1. **Hook** (1-2 sentences, before the fold): The strongest claim or observation
2. **Context** (2-4 sentences): Why this matters, what problem it solves
3. **Evidence** (2-4 sentences or a short list): Numbers, code example, comparison
4. **Takeaway** (1-2 sentences): What the reader should do or think
5. **CTA** (1 sentence + link): Comment prompt, link in first comment, or direct link

## Code and Technical Content

- Code blocks are supported but render as plain text — use them sparingly
- Screenshots of code (with syntax highlighting) perform better than inline code
- Keep technical depth accessible: explain what the code does before showing it
- Longer code snippets are acceptable vs. Twitter — LinkedIn audience expects more depth

## Links and Reach

- **Posts with links get significantly less reach** on LinkedIn (algorithm suppresses external links)
- Best practice: put the link in the first comment, reference it in the post ("link in comments")
- Carousel posts (multi-image) get high reach — consider for visual technical content
- Video posts also get boosted reach

## Hashtags

- Use 3-5 relevant hashtags, placed at the end of the post
- LinkedIn hashtags are functional — people follow them
- Relevant: `#Python`, `#AIEngineering`, `#OpenSource`, `#MachineLearning`, `#IBMResearch`
- Don't use more than 5 — it looks like spam

## Engagement Patterns

- Posts that end with a question get more comments
- Polls get engagement but attract low-quality signals — use selectively
- "Hot take" or contrarian framing gets shares; evidence-backed framing gets saves
- Tagging colleagues who contributed is appropriate and expands reach to their networks

## Formatting Tips

- Line breaks matter: single blank lines between sections improve readability
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max) perform better than dense blocks
- Bullet lists work well for 3-7 items; anything more should be a carousel or blog post
