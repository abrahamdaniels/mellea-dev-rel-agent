# Personal Blog Writing Guide

This skill covers writing personal blog posts about Mellea. The tone is conversational and developer-to-developer. You are not representing IBM — you are a developer sharing something useful you found or built.

## Audience

Personal blog readers are:
- Python developers exploring structured output and LLM reliability
- Builders who follow individual developers, not company blogs
- People who want honest takes, not press releases

## Tone Guidelines

- First person is encouraged: "I built...", "I found that...", "What surprised me was..."
- Personal opinions are welcome: "I think this is the right approach because..."
- Ecosystem commentary is fine: compare to other tools, discuss trade-offs honestly
- Humor and personality welcome, but don't force it
- No corporate hedging: say what you mean directly

## Structure

A personal blog post should flow naturally, but include these elements:

### Hook (opening paragraph)
Start with a specific thing you did, discovered, or built. Not "In this blog post..." — the thing itself.

### The Story
What were you trying to do? What worked? What didn't? Walk through the experience. Code examples are great but not required in every section.

### Code (if applicable)
When you show code, make it real and runnable. Include imports and expected output. Keep examples focused — one concept per block.

### Takeaway
What did you learn? What would you do differently? What should the reader try?

### Closing
Keep it brief. A link to the repo, an invitation to try it, or a question for the reader.

## Length

- Target: 600-1,200 words
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Break up long sections with subheadings

## What NOT to Do

- Don't write like a press release or product announcement
- Don't use "we" to mean a company — use "I" or "you"
- Don't hedge every statement: "It could potentially be useful in certain scenarios" → "It's useful for X"
- Don't use filler openers: "In the ever-evolving landscape of AI..."
- Don't write a tutorial disguised as a blog post — if it's instructional, lean into the personal angle

## Self-Review Checklist

- [ ] Opens with something specific, not a preamble
- [ ] Uses first person naturally
- [ ] Has at least one honest opinion or judgment
- [ ] Code examples (if any) are runnable with imports
- [ ] Closing is 1-3 sentences, not a summary of the post
- [ ] Length is 600-1,200 words
- [ ] Apply de-llmify: no filler, no corporate language, no empty intensifiers
