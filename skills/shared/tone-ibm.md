# IBM Tone Guidelines

Use this tone when writing on behalf of IBM Research or the IBM organization. Professional, authoritative, and focused on enterprise value and research credibility.

## Voice Characteristics

- **First person plural**: "We built", "Our team", "IBM Research has"
- **Professional but not stiff**: Clear, direct sentences. Not jargon-heavy.
- **Research-credible**: Emphasize methodology, rigor, reproducibility
- **Enterprise-relevant**: Connect to real-world deployability, scale, reliability
- **Inclusive**: "developers using our tools", not exclusionary language

## What to Do

- Lead with the capability and its business/research relevance
- Reference IBM Research or the team when crediting the work
- Connect to broader IBM themes: enterprise AI, responsible AI, hybrid cloud
- Use "we" consistently throughout the piece
- Include a clear call to action (try it, read the paper, contribute)

## What to Avoid

- "I" — always "we" or "our team" in IBM voice
- Informal contractions in formal contexts (blog posts, LinkedIn)
- Overpromising: don't claim things the library doesn't do yet
- Burying the lead with background — start with the capability
- Vague enterprise language without substance ("innovative solutions", "transformative")

## IBM-Specific Messaging Angles

1. **Reliability for enterprise AI**: Production systems need predictable outputs — Mellea provides that
2. **Open source commitment**: IBM Research builds in the open; link to GitHub
3. **Granite model family alignment**: Mellea works well with IBM's Granite models
4. **Research-to-production path**: From research prototype to deployable library

## Example Transformations

**Before (personal):**
> I built Mellea because I was tired of LLM outputs breaking my JSON parsers.

**After (IBM):**
> IBM Research developed Mellea to address a common reliability challenge in production AI systems: LLM outputs that fail schema validation. With Mellea, teams can define expected output structures using standard Pydantic models and rely on automatic retry logic to handle edge cases.

## Platform-Specific Notes

- **LinkedIn**: Full professional register. 2-4 paragraphs. End with a link and hashtags.
- **Twitter/X**: Can be slightly more concise, but keep "we" voice and professional framing.
